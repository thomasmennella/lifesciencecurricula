Critical path & advising notes
  • BIO 215 (fall only) is the first major bottleneck. It requires BIO 108+118 (Y1 spring) and CHEM 106 or CHEM 111 (concurrent ok). CHEM 111 is fall only, so the standard path is: CHEM 105 (Y1 spring) → CHEM 111 (Y2 fall, concurrent with BIO 215). Delaying BIO 107/117 or CHEM 105 out of Y1 pushes BIO 215 to Y3 fall at earliest.
  • CHEM 111 vs CHEM 106: CHEM 111 is the standard HST path (fall only). CHEM 106 is the Health Sciences/pre-Pharmacy path (spring only). Students cannot receive credit for both. Decide early — switching paths later is costly.
  • BIO 216 (spring only) requires BIO 215 (fall only). Since BIO 215 is earliest in Y2 fall, BIO 216 cannot start before Y2 spring. BIO 216 is a prerequisite for most upper-level HS electives (HS 315, 320, 321, 310, 330).
  • Statistics (MATH 120/121) is a prerequisite for HS 325 (Epidemiology). Taking it in Y2 spring (as suggested) opens HS 325 for Y3 onward.
  • PSY elective sequencing: Two PSY 3XX courses required. PSY 326 (Abnormal Psychology) also requires junior standing. Plan PSY elective choices by Y2 to ensure prerequisites are met.
  • HS 322 (Immunology) requires CHEM 209, which requires CHEM 106 — not in the standard HST core (which uses CHEM 111). Consult advisor before planning this elective.
  • HS 412 (Cancer Biology) requires BIO 306 (Genetics), not in the HST core. Instructor permission is the practical path for HST students.
  • HS 471 (capstone, 3 cr) is distinct from HS 470 (Health Sciences capstone, 1 cr). HS 471 is larger and more substantial. No hard prerequisites, but Y4 spring placement implies senior standing.
  • This program does NOT fulfill all PharmD prerequisite requirements. Students considering pharmacy school should follow the Health Sciences curriculum instead.
  • Major GPA: 2.0 minimum across all HS, BIO, CHEM, and PSY courses pursued as part of the degree. Total credits: 120. Major core: 60 cr. WIC: 6 cr (one 200/300-level + one 300/400-level). No transfer credit for major-level science lab courses taught online.
