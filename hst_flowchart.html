Critical path & advising notes
  • BIO 215 (fall only) is the first major bottleneck. It requires BIO 108+118 (spring Y1) and CHEM 106 or CHEM 111 (concurrent ok). Since CHEM 111 is fall only and requires CHEM 105, the standard path is: CHEM 105 (Y1 spring) → CHEM 111 (Y2 fall, concurrent with BIO 215). Delaying CHEM 105 or BIO 107/117 out of Y1 pushes BIO 215 to Y3 fall at the earliest.
  • CHEM 111 vs CHEM 106: CHEM 111 is the standard Health Studies path (fall only; broader, health-career-focused). CHEM 106 is used by Health Sciences and pre-Pharmacy students (spring only; more rigorous). Students cannot receive credit for both. Students considering switching to Health Sciences or pursuing pharmacy should take CHEM 106 instead.
  • BIO 216 (spring only) requires BIO 215. Since BIO 215 is fall only, BIO 216 cannot start until Y2 spring at the earliest. BIO 216 is a prerequisite for most upper-level HS electives (HS 315, 320, 321, 310, 330).
  • Statistics requirement (MATH 120 or 121): Placed in Y2 spring per the suggested sequence. Earlier is better — MATH 120/121 is a prerequisite for HS 325 (Epidemiology), and statistical literacy supports lab and research work across the major.
  • PSY elective sequencing: Two PSY 3XX courses are required. PSY 326 (Abnormal Psychology) also requires junior standing. Both electives are spread across Y3 spring and Y4 fall in the suggested sequence — plan course selections by Y2 to ensure prerequisites are met.
  • HS 322 (Immunology) requires CHEM 209 (Organic Chemistry I), which is not in the Health Studies core and requires CHEM 106 as a prerequisite. Health Studies students who want this elective must deviate from the standard CHEM 111 path — consult advisor early.
  • HS 412 (Cancer Biology) requires BIO 306 (Genetics), also not in the Health Studies core. Instructor permission is the practical path for Health Studies students.
  • HS 471 (Seminar — capstone, 3 cr) is distinct from HS 470 (the Health Sciences capstone, 1 cr). HS 471 is larger and more substantial. No hard prerequisites listed in the catalog, but it is placed in Y4 spring; senior standing is implied.
  • This program is NOT a path to the WNE PharmD. The catalog explicitly states that the Health Studies curriculum does not fulfill all PharmD prerequisite requirements. Students considering pharmacy school should follow the Health Sciences curriculum instead.
  • Major GPA: 2.0 minimum across all HS, BIO, CHEM, and PSY courses pursued as part of the degree. Total credits: 120. Major core: 60 cr. WIC: 6 cr required (one 200/300-level, one 300/400-level). No transfer credit for major-level science lab courses taught online.
