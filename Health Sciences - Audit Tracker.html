Critical path & advising notes
  • The CHEM chain is the longest bottleneck. CHEM 105 (Y1F) → CHEM 106 (Y1Sp, C− min) → CHEM 209 (Y2F, C− min) → CHEM 210 (Y2Sp) → CHEM 314/324 (Y4Sp). Missing or failing CHEM 105 in Year 1 pushes Biochemistry out of the four-year plan entirely.
  • Grade minimums on the CHEM chain: C− is required to advance from CHEM 105→106 and CHEM 106→209. No minimum grade is stated in the catalog for subsequent steps, though academic standing requirements still apply overall.
  • BIO 306 (Genetics) is the central prerequisite hub. It requires CHEM 210 (earliest: Y2 spring), making Y3 its earliest possible semester. It then gates HS 470 (capstone) and BIO 401 (elective). Any delay here delays graduation.
  • BIO 215 (fall only) requires BIO 108+118 and CHEM 106 (concurrent ok). Delaying BIO 107 or CHEM 105 out of Y1 fall pushes BIO 215 to Y3 fall at the earliest, cascading into BIO 216 and BIO 306.
  • PSY 326 requires PSY 101 plus junior standing. Even if PSY 101 is taken in Y2 fall (earliest practical), PSY 326 cannot be completed before Y3 at the earliest.
  • CHEM 402 (Toxicology) requires CHEM 314+324 as prerequisites — courses not completed until Y4 spring. Since CHEM 402 is fall only, it is inaccessible within a standard four-year degree.
  • BIO 306 semester discrepancy: the course description states "fall only," but the suggested sequence in the catalog places BIO 306 in Y3 spring. Confirm semester availability with the department before planning around it.
  • MATH 123 → PHYS 123: MATH 123 completes Y1 fall, so PHYS 123 is technically eligible from Y2 fall onward — earlier than the Y3 fall placement in the suggested sequence.
  • Major GPA requirement: 2.0 minimum across all HS, BIO (excl. BIO 15X/19X), and CHEM courses. Total program: 120 cr. Major core: 76 cr. Writing Intensive: 6 cr (one 200/300-level + one 300/400-level). CHEM 314 and CHEM 324 both satisfy the upper WIC requirement.
  • No transfer credit is accepted for major-level science lab courses taught online.
